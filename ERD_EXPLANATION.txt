The USER entity is the main account holder in the system. It represents the person who logs in and uses the platform. The USER entity is connected to the BUSINESS_PROFILE entity, the SOCIAL_ACCOUNT entity, the SUBSCRIPTION entity, and the NOTIFICATION entity. This means that each account can own a business profile, connect social media accounts, choose a subscription plan, and receive system notifications.

The BUSINESS_PROFILE entity stores the business information needed by the system. It contains details such as business name, description, location, target audience, and logo. This entity is connected to the INDUSTRY entity, the CATEGORY entity, the BRAND_SETTING entity, the CAMPAIGN entity, the CONTENT_ITEM entity, the CALENDAR_POST entity, the ANALYTICS_SNAPSHOT entity, the MISSED_OPPORTUNITY entity, and the HOLIDAY_CAMPAIGN entity. This shows that the business profile is the central record that supports personalization, content generation, scheduling, analytics, and holiday planning.

The INDUSTRY entity represents the broad business field selected during onboarding, such as food and beverage, retail, beauty, technology, health and fitness, hospitality, creative services, education, automotive, or other industries. Each industry can contain multiple categories through the CATEGORY entity. The CATEGORY entity stores more specific business types that belong to a selected industry. This relationship allows the system to suggest appropriate business categories based on the chosen industry.

The BRAND_SETTING entity stores the brand identity of the business. It contains the brand tone, marketing goal, and selected theme. This entity helps the AI understand how the business wants to communicate with its audience, whether the tone is professional, friendly, luxury, trendy, or fun. It is linked directly to the BUSINESS_PROFILE entity because every business profile needs its own branding preferences.

The SOCIAL_ACCOUNT entity stores connected social media accounts such as Facebook and Instagram. It contains the platform name, account handle, and status of the connection. The USER entity is connected to multiple SOCIAL_ACCOUNT records, which means a single user can connect more than one platform. This supports multi-platform publishing from one dashboard.

The SUBSCRIPTION entity manages the plan selected by the user. It stores the plan name, billing cycle, price, status, and dates related to the subscription. This entity is connected to the USER entity because each user can have a subscription record that defines the available features and access level in the system.

The CAMPAIGN entity represents marketing campaigns created by the business. It stores the campaign name, status, start date, and end date. The BUSINESS_PROFILE entity can run multiple campaigns, and each campaign can include multiple content items. This relationship helps organize posts into larger marketing efforts such as promotions, seasonal sales, and launch campaigns.

The CONTENT_ITEM entity stores AI-generated or manually created marketing content. It includes the title, caption, hashtags, call to action, image prompt, and status of the content. This entity is connected to the BUSINESS_PROFILE entity because all content belongs to a specific business. It is also connected to the CAMPAIGN entity, the CALENDAR_POST entity, the PUBLISHED_POST entity, the HOLIDAY_CAMPAIGN entity, and the RECOVERY_PLAN entity. This means content can be created for campaigns, scheduled on the calendar, published to social media, inspired by holiday campaigns, or suggested as part of a recovery plan.

The CALENDAR_POST entity stores scheduled posts in the content calendar. It records the scheduled date and time and the current status of the post. This entity is linked to the BUSINESS_PROFILE entity and the CONTENT_ITEM entity. The relationship shows that each scheduled post belongs to a business and is based on a specific content item.

The PUBLISHED_POST entity stores posts that have already been published to connected social accounts. It includes the publish date, platform post ID, and status. The CONTENT_ITEM entity is connected to the PUBLISHED_POST entity because a content item can eventually become a live post. The SOCIAL_ACCOUNT entity is also connected to PUBLISHED_POST because the same content can be published to one or more social media platforms.

The ANALYTICS_SNAPSHOT entity stores performance data over time. It includes total posts, scheduled posts, engagement rate, reach, followers growth, missed promotions, and the date when the data was captured. This entity is connected to the BUSINESS_PROFILE entity and the CAMPAIGN entity. This allows the system to monitor performance for each business and campaign.

The HOLIDAY_CAMPAIGN entity stores seasonal campaign ideas and holiday-based marketing plans. It includes the holiday name, campaign title, prediction, and event date. This entity is connected to the BUSINESS_PROFILE entity because each business can plan multiple holiday campaigns. Holiday campaigns can also inspire new content items, helping the AI generator create timely promotional material.

The MISSED_OPPORTUNITY entity stores missed promotional posts or delayed marketing opportunities. It includes the title, severity, status, and missed date. This entity is connected to the BUSINESS_PROFILE entity because each business may have its own missed opportunities. It is also connected to the RECOVERY_PLAN entity so the system can suggest corrective actions.

The RECOVERY_PLAN entity stores recommendations that help the business recover from a missed opportunity. It includes the recommendation text, status, and creation date. This entity is connected to the MISSED_OPPORTUNITY entity and can suggest new CONTENT_ITEM records for recovery content.

The NOTIFICATION entity manages reminders, alerts, and system messages. It stores the notification type, title, message, unread status, and creation date. This entity is connected to the USER entity and can reference the CALENDAR_POST entity, the PUBLISHED_POST entity, or the MISSED_OPPORTUNITY entity. This allows the system to notify the user about scheduled posts, published content, and recovery alerts.
